Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our 6-Week Intensive Program

What is the duration of the program?

The program runs for 6 weeks, Monday through Friday, with daily sessions from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Sundays are dedicated to comprehensive problem-solving sessions.

Who can register for this program?

The program is open to:

  • CBU students (for academic credit)
  • External students and professionals (open enrollment)
  • Anyone interested in learning mathematical methods and economics

What are the prerequisites?

Basic knowledge of high school mathematics (algebra, functions) is recommended. No prior economics knowledge is required - we start from the fundamentals.

Is there a certificate upon completion?

Yes! All participants who complete the program requirements (attendance, assignments, and final assessment) will receive a Certificate of Completion from the CBU Mathematics & Economics Department.

How do I register for the program?

You can register through the registration form on our homepage. After submission, you'll receive a confirmation email with further instructions.

What is the registration deadline?

Registration remains open throughout the program, but we recommend registering at least one week before the start date to ensure access to all materials.

Is there a registration fee?

For CBU students: No fee (covered by tuition)
For external participants: A nominal fee applies for certification and resource access. Contact us for details.

Can I register for only one course?

Yes, you can register for either Mathematical Methods 1 or Economics 110 separately, though we recommend taking both for maximum benefit.

How is the program assessed?

Assessment includes:

  • Weekly tests (30%)
  • Daily assignments (20%)
  • Capstone project (25%)
  • Final examination (25%)

What support is available for struggling students?

We provide:

  • Daily office hours with instructors
  • Peer study groups
  • Supplementary materials
  • One-on-one tutoring sessions
  • Online forum support

Are there any required textbooks?

No textbooks are required to purchase. All necessary materials (notes, problems, solutions) are provided through our online platform. Recommended readings will be suggested for those wanting additional resources.

What happens if I miss classes?

All lectures and materials are recorded and available online. However, consistent attendance is strongly encouraged for optimal learning. Minimum 80% attendance is required for certification.

What resources are provided?

Each registered student receives:

  • Complete lecture notes for all chapters
  • Practice problem sets with solutions
  • Video tutorials and explanations
  • Access to Google Drive with all materials
  • Interactive problem-solving tools

How do I access the learning materials?

After registration, you'll receive login credentials for our online platform. All materials are organized by week and day, following the program schedule.

Are the materials downloadable?

Yes, all notes and problem sets are available in PDF format for download. Video content can be streamed online.

Can I share the materials with others?

Materials are for personal educational use only. Sharing with registered study groups is permitted, but distribution to non-registered individuals is prohibited.

What technology do I need?

You'll need:

  • A computer or tablet with internet access
  • Basic calculator (scientific calculator recommended)
  • PDF reader
  • Video streaming capability

Is there a mobile app?

Our website is fully mobile-responsive. While we don't have a dedicated app, you can access all features through your mobile browser. You can also add the site to your home screen for app-like access.

What if I have technical issues?

Contact our technical support team at techsupport@cbu.ac.zm. We typically respond within 24 hours. Common issues and solutions are also documented in our help section.

Do I need special software?

No special software is required. All materials are in standard formats (PDF, MP4). For mathematical notation, we recommend using a standard web browser with JavaScript enabled.
