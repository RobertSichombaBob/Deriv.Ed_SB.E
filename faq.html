General Questions

What is the duration of the 6-week program?

The program runs for exactly 6 weeks, Monday through Friday, with two daily sessions: 12:00-13:45 and 17:00-18:45. Sunday problem-solving sessions are available for extra practice.

Who can register for this program?

The program is open to:

  • CBU students seeking credit for Mathematical Methods 1 and/or Economics 110
  • External university students looking for intensive preparation
  • Professionals wanting to refresh their mathematical and economic skills
  • Anyone interested in mastering university-level mathematics and economics

What are the prerequisites for the program?

For Mathematics: Basic algebra knowledge (high school level)
For Economics: No prerequisites required - we start from fundamentals
Recommended: Familiarity with basic mathematical concepts and willingness to learn.

Mathematics Medium Difficulty

Problem 1: Solving Linear Equations

Solve for x: \( 3(2x - 5) + 4 = 7x - 2(x + 1) \)

$$3(2x - 5) + 4 = 7x - 2(x + 1)$$

Solution:

$$\begin{aligned} 3(2x - 5) + 4 &= 7x - 2(x + 1) \\ 6x - 15 + 4 &= 7x - 2x - 2 \\ 6x - 11 &= 5x - 2 \\ 6x - 5x &= -2 + 11 \\ x &= 9 \end{aligned}$$

Answer: \( x = 9 \)

Mathematics Hard Difficulty

Problem 2: Word Problem

A company produces two products. Product A requires 2 hours of labor and 3 units of material, while Product B requires 3 hours of labor and 2 units of material. If the company has 120 hours of labor and 150 units of material available, and they want to maximize production given that they must produce at least 10 units of Product A, how many units of each should they produce?

Solution: This is a linear programming problem. Let \( x \) = units of Product A, \( y \) = units of Product B.

$$\begin{aligned} \text{Constraints:} & \\ 2x + 3y &\leq 120 \quad \text{(Labor)} \\ 3x + 2y &\leq 150 \quad \text{(Material)} \\ x &\geq 10 \\ x, y &\geq 0 \\ \text{Objective:} & \quad \text{Maximize } P = x + y \end{aligned}$$

Solve graphically or using corner point method.

Chapter 1: Economic Fundamentals

Economics Easy Difficulty

Problem 1: Production Possibility Frontier

An economy can produce either 100 units of food or 50 units of clothing. Draw the PPF. What is the opportunity cost of 1 unit of clothing? If the economy is currently producing 60 units of food, how much clothing can it produce?

Solution:

1. PPF: Vertical intercept = 100 units of food, Horizontal intercept = 50 units of clothing

2. Opportunity cost of 1 clothing = \( \frac{100}{50} = 2 \) units of food

3. At 60 food: \( \frac{60}{100} = 0.6 \) of resources used for food, remaining \( 0.4 \) for clothing: \( 0.4 \times 50 = 20 \) units of clothing

Answer: Opportunity cost = 2 food per clothing; At 60 food, can produce 20 clothing

Economics Medium Difficulty

Problem 2: Comparative Advantage

Country A can produce 10 tons of wheat or 5 tons of corn per worker. Country B can produce 8 tons of wheat or 4 tons of corn per worker. Which country has comparative advantage in wheat production? Should they trade?

Solution:

Country A opportunity cost: 1 wheat = \( \frac{5}{10} = 0.5 \) corn; 1 corn = \( \frac{10}{5} = 2 \) wheat

Country B opportunity cost: 1 wheat = \( \frac{4}{8} = 0.5 \) corn; 1 corn = \( \frac{8}{4} = 2 \) wheat

Both have same opportunity costs, so no comparative advantage exists. Trade wouldn't benefit either country based on these numbers.
