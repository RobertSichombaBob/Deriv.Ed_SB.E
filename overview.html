Program Overview

Complete coverage of Mathematical Methods 1 and Economics 110

Mathematical Methods 1

Chapters Covered:

Ch 5 Equations, Inequalities, and Problem Solving
Ch 6 Quadratic Equations
Ch 7 Inequalities Equations
Ch 9 Quadratic Functions
Ch 10 Polynomial Functions and Equations
Ch 12 Arithmetic and Geometric Sequences
Ch 14 Partial Fractions
Ch 15 Combination, Permutation and Binomial Expansions
Ch 16 Matrices
Ch 17 Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Learning Objectives:

  • Master algebraic techniques and problem-solving strategies
  • Understand function analysis and graphing
  • Solve complex equations and inequalities
  • Apply mathematical concepts to real-world problems
  • Develop proficiency in matrix operations
  • Understand sequences, series, and mathematical induction

Resources Provided:

Detailed Notes
Practice Problems
Solution Guides
Video Tutorials

Economics 110

Chapters Covered:

Ch 1 The Nature of Economics
Ch 2 Demand, Supply and Market Equilibrium
Ch 3 Elasticity
Ch 4 Consumer Theory
Ch 5 Production and Costs

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand economic principles and systems
  • Analyze market forces of demand and supply
  • Calculate and interpret various elasticities
  • Understand consumer behavior and utility maximization
  • Analyze production functions and cost structures
  • Apply economic concepts to real-world scenarios

Topics Include:

Opportunity Cost PPF Analysis Market Equilibrium Price Elasticity Utility Theory Cost Curves Economies of Scale Market Structures
